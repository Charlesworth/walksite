Endless Leg Day

Countdown to Cajon
34.312572,-117.478125

1/05/2016


April 30th was pretty hum drum, we woke to light rain and the sound of a freight train echoing through the hills. We set off with the intention to go 15 miles which would leave us 6 miles to Cajon Pass McDonald's the next day. Today's scenery was different in that it was a lot more industrial, we passed by another damm and also some kind of factory, all the while we were serenaded by the whistle of the unseen train. We reached out goal of 15 miles and camped at the foot of 3 hills with our tent door facing the valley. The clouds were turning dark grey and the wind picked up, we almost crapped ourselves when a massive gust tore one of the corner pegs from the ground. If we hadn't been sitting in the tent I think it would have been ripped away entirety! The peg was long gone but luckily our poo hole trowel is just a big plastic peg so we substituted it. Charlie used his engineering skills to secure the rest of the pegs with copious amounts of rocks. We settled down anxious about our tent but it held up and in the morning we woke eager to hike towards those golden arches!

The 6 miles to the pass was very up and down and up and down, we contoured each hill to such an extent that I couldn't tell which bits were ahead or behind. We finally made it and was greeted with a six-pack of Cors beer left by the side of the trail with "trail magic" scrawled on the box. With a beer in hand we walked up the unfamiliar concreted road to McDonald's and civilization. I didn't eat as much as I expected, though we did go 2 rounds of breakfast and regular menu, the vanilla milkshake was devine. We had made a reservation the day before for the Best Western Hotel and after digesting a little we ambled up there to await our room - yay a shower and a bed!
