Endless Leg Day

Not so endless leg day
36.125584, -115.141893

04/05/2016


Kady and I have decided to leave the trail for now, 340 miles of desert was enough for us. After around 22 days on the trail, our bodies were getting well adjusted, our feet were holding up and the heat was manageable. The main issue we found was boredom, we both found it was getting harder and harder to put the miles in each day.

The trail is very rugged, so this means that 99% of your walking day you need to be looking at the 2 meters in front of you. The first week we were taken aback by the scenery and foot pain, the second week we needed the help of music to get us through the day and the third week brought talking books. We were loving the people, camping and sights, basically everything but the walking.

It's with a stupid sense of hindsight that we have found we just don’t enjoy hiking enough to be doing it non stop for five and a half months. While we feel a little bit silly, who were we to know that long distance through hiking wasn’t for us, there was only one way to find out and that was on the trail. We certainly gave it a good go, but have decided that we only have a finite amount of time and money to enjoy our quarter life retirement.

So what will we do now? Well, we’ve spent the last few days split between LA and Las Vegas getting used to beds and warm food again. We’re going to do a bit of exploring the US by car and then decide if we want to do more of the trail once the Sierras open up in a month or so.

We’re currently driving to the Grand Canyon to do a two day hike through it and after that will be making our way up to Santa Rosa to house sit with two dogs and a cat for eight days. Our schedule is pretty flexible so if anyone has any suggestions on cool things to do or see let us know. I’m sure Kady will continue blogging and we both will be taking plenty of pictures so you will all find out how it goes.
