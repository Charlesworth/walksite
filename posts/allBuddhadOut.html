Endless Leg Day

All Buddha'd out
13.727666,100.525585

23/05/2016


Today we decided was temple day. We woke up at 4am due to jet lag / time zone adjustment and after watching the newest game of thrones episode, we went to the reception to book for a second night. There is a small street market a couple of streets from the hostel and we walked there to pick up some breakfast. We joined the biggest que (more like a crowd as no one que's here!) in front of a small stall and pointed to the dishes we wanted. We ended up with some Thai green curry with crispy fried chicken, some weird sweet dish which had colourful jelly and fruit - this was served in a plastic bag which despite my doubts held the milky liquid in and some Thai custard with Durian fruit. The curry was great, the jelly was pretty good and the custard was a bit weird but I want to try it again to make up my mind.

We then headed to the Grand Palace via the river ferry. The palace was unsurprisingly crowded but this didn't stop us from enjoying the beautiful and very golden architecture. The details in the painted frescos was amazing. We then walked to Wat Pho, the temple of the reclining Buddha. During our short walk we were shouted to by lots of tut tut drivers offering rides, but we walked on ignoring them and there rainbow coloured cabs. The reclining Buddhais a 45 foot long golden statue which is laying on its side with its head on a couple of golden jewel encrusted, but uncomfy looking pillows. It was pretty breathtaking seeing the level of detail. We rambled around the temple grounds and read some info about Buddhism for a while longer before heading back to the hostel. We were bushed after such a long day and chilled out for the rest of the evening.
