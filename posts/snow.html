Endless Leg Day

Snow
34.357983,-117.317241

28/04/2016


Mile 293 to 305

Despite camping at 5,500 feet we woke up in the night to hammering on the tent, which we learnt upon waking was snow. It wasn't a very comfortable night, our tent has two single layer walls and if Charlie or I brush against then when wet then moisture will start to drip through. We didn't realise it during the night, but the snow weighed down our tent walls and reduced the space we had to sleep in which led to us touching the forbidden sides and getting wet. It was snowing / hailing when we woke and packing up was a miserable affair. Made more so by the fact that as soon as we pack the tent the inside of it will get wet through because we don't have a separate rain fly. We were both freezing (especially our fingers) and after gobbling some Granola had to get moving to warm up. We had a small bit of trail magic in the form of a single use hand warmer which a nice man named Daniel had given to me on the bus to Big Bear. We activated it and took turns clutching it in our hands as we walked.

We hiked fast and soon found ourselves in sandy scub again. The day was overcast but as soon as there was a slight break in the clouds we spread our tent and wet gear on some rocks to dry out. The day wore on and though we were shooting for mile 307 (Deep Creek Hot Springs) we pulled up at 305 were there was a gorgeous Oasis of a camp site with a trickling stream, sandy beach and good rocks for perching on. We even managed to find a grassy spot for our tent, though some inventiveness with the awning was required.

It's amazing how different the start of a day can be compared to the end. In the morning we were freezing our asses off and stressing out about sleeping in a wet tent in possible rain and by evening we were snug in a dream spot laughing and joking with other hikers.
